Butter Chicken

India

Dish History & Significance

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is one of the most famous dishes in Indian cuisine. It was created in the 1950s at Moti Mahal, a restaurant in Delhi, India. The dish was invented by Kundan Lal Gujral, Kundan Lal Jaggi, and Thakur Dass, who were Punjabi refugees from Peshawar (now in Pakistan) after the Partition of India in 1947.The dish was born out of necessity—at Moti Mahal, they would cook tandoori chicken in large batches, and to avoid waste, they repurposed leftover chicken by simmering it in a creamy tomato-based gravy with butter and spices. This innovation led to the creation of Butter Chicken, which quickly became a favorite among locals and foreigners alike.
